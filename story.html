C.K. Heath's Story

C. K. Heath was born and raised in Georgia and remains active community where he and his family lives.

A Third Generation Teacher

Heath was raised in Woodbury, Georgia. In 1987, he entered Augusta College and was lead to Trinity CME Church were he served as an active young adult as a member of the choir and VBS teacher.

A Grateful Leader

Heath reached full connection and was assigned to Jones Tabernacle CME of Macon, Georgia and then went on to pastor in the First and Fifth Episcopal districts before being assigned to his current role as pastor of Carter Metropolitan CME Church in Fort Worth, Texas in 2014. He has promoted school partnerships, One Church One School an directed a Freedom School program…

Always Giving Back

Heath returned to Phillips School of Theology Alumni Association, as president, where he just completed his 5th year. Heath has also been Board Member of Housing Management and Tarrant Churches Together.

Heath graduated from International Theological Seminary in 2016 with a Doctorate of Theology. In his spare time, Heath coaching little league basketball and spending time with his family.
