C. K. Heath's Story

C. K. Heath was born and raised in Georgia and he is engaged and active member of the community where he lives.

A CME Leader of his Generation

Heath was raised in Woodbury, Georgia. In 1986, while a student at Augusta College, he was lead to Trinity CME Church where he served as a active choir member and VBS teacher. As a minister in Full Connection, he has pastored in the first, fifth and sixth districts before serving his present assignment as a grateful pastor of Carter Metropolitan in the 8th Episcopal CME district.

Officer and a Servant of the Lord

As a Commissioned Army Officer ROTC, he completed his Bachelor of Business Administration-management in 1992. He went on to complete Army Chaplaincy School before resigning his commission to serve as a pastor. He has earned his Master of Divinity and Master of Christian Education at Phillips School of Theology at the Interdenominational Theological Center. He also earned a Doctorate of Theology in 2016 from International Theological Seminary.

Teaching in The Bronx

In 1998, John returned to his alma mater, as a History and Government teacher, where he just completed his 22nd year. John has also been the Alumni Coordinator, Director of Development, the History Department Chairperson, and the Recruiting Coordinator, a role he now handles in addition to his teaching duties. John also serves as the cantor at school Masses, held on Catholic Holy Days of obligation.

John graduated from Fordham in 1993 with a Bachelor of Arts in Media Studies, and a minor in Political Science. In his spare time, John enjoys singing with TIM CURTIN and FRIENDS, a local band in the Bronx. He also enjoys swimming and kayak. John resides in City Island, and his two grown sons, Nicholas and Matthew, live in Nanuet, NY.
